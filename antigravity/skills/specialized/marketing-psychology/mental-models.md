# Marketing Mental Models & Biases

A comprehensive list of cognitive biases and psychological principles to apply to marketing strategy.

## Key Cognitive Biases

### The Framing Effect
People react differently to a particular choice depending on how it's presented.
- **Example**: "Saves you $100" vs. "Costs $100 less."
- **Marketing Tip**: Use negative framing ("Don't lose out") for high-stakes items and positive framing ("Save more") for routine benefits.

### The Decoy Effect
When consumers change their preference between two options when a third, dominated option is introduced.
- **Example**: Small popcorn ($3) vs. Large popcorn ($7). Most buy small. Add Medium ($6.50). Most buy Large because it's "only 50 cents more" than the Medium.
- **Marketing Tip**: Use a "decoy" plan in your pricing table to nudge users toward the most profitable option.

### The Zeigarnik Effect
People remember uncompleted tasks better than completed ones.
- **Example**: Progress bars, incomplete profiles, "5 steps to completion."
- **Marketing Tip**: Give users a small "win" early in a process to keep them motivated to finish.

### The Halo Effect
Our overall impression of a person/brand influences how we feel and think about their character.
- **Example**: If we like a brand's visual design, we assume their software is stable.
- **Marketing Tip**: Invest heavily in the first touchpoint (design, speed) to create a positive "halo" for the rest of the customer experience.

### Analysis Paralysis (Hick's Law)
The more choices you give a user, the longer it takes for them to make a decision.
- **Example**: A landing page with 10 different CTAs.
- **Marketing Tip**: Limit choices to a single primary CTA or no more than 3 pricing tiers.

## Persuasion Principles (Beyond Cialdini)

### The Pygmalion Effect
High expectations lead to improved performance in a given area.
- **Example**: Telling a new user they are in the "Top 1% of efficient creators" motivates them to use the tool more.
- **Marketing Tip**: Use affirmations and high-status labels for your power users.

### The Von Restorff Effect (Isolation Effect)
Multiple similar objects are present, the one that differs from the rest is most likely to be remembered.
- **Example**: Every competitor uses blue; you use high-contrast orange.
- **Marketing Tip**: Ensure your primary CTA button is a unique color not used elsewhere on the page.

### The Baader-Meinhof Phenomenon (Frequency Illusion)
After noticing something for the first time, you start seeing it everywhere.
- **Example**: A user sees an ad for YourApp, then starts noticing YourApp mentioned in blog posts.
- **Marketing Tip**: Use retargeting ads and coordinated launches to create this "everywhere at once" feeling for your target audience.

---

## Decision Fatigue
The deteriorating quality of decisions made by an individual after a long session of decision making.
- **Example**: Asking for 20 preferences during an onboarding flow.
- **Marketing Tip**: Save complex decisions for later, or use default settings to reduce the load on the user.

---

## Social Identity Theory
People maintain their self-esteem through their group memberships.
- **Example**: "Join 5,000+ other indie hackers."
- **Marketing Tip**: Create a strong "Us vs. Them" identity that users want to be a part of.
