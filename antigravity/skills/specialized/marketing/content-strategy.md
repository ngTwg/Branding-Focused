## Overview

Expert frameworks for creating and editing high-converting marketing content. Includes the Seven Sweeps editing framework, copywriting principles, and content structure guidelines.

---

## Rules

**RULE-001: Clarity Over Cleverness**
If you have to choose between clear and creative, choose clear. Every sentence should have one job. Remove words that don't add meaning.

**RULE-002: Benefits Over Features**
Always connect features to outcomes. Features = what it does. Benefits = what that means for the customer.

```markdown
❌ "Our platform uses AI-powered analytics"
✅ "Our AI-powered analytics surface insights you'd miss manually—so you can make better decisions in half the time"
```

**RULE-003: The Seven Sweeps Framework**
Edit copy through seven sequential passes:
1. **Clarity** - Can the reader understand?
2. **Voice & Tone** - Is it consistent?
3. **So What** - Does every claim answer "why should I care?"
4. **Prove It** - Is every claim supported with evidence?
5. **Specificity** - Is the copy concrete enough?
6. **Heightened Emotion** - Does it make the reader feel something?
7. **Zero Risk** - Have we removed every barrier to action?

**RULE-004: Specificity Beats Vagueness**
Replace vague language with concrete details:
- "Save time" → "Save 4 hours every week"
- "Many customers" → "2,847 teams"
- "Fast results" → "Results in 14 days"

**RULE-005: Prove Every Claim**
Support assertions with testimonials, case studies, statistics, third-party validation, guarantees, or review scores. Never use "best" or "leading" without evidence.

**RULE-006: One Idea Per Section**
Follow the "Rule of One": one main idea per section, one audience per page, one clear CTA.

**RULE-007: Write to the Reader**
Use "you" language. Speak directly to the reader's problem or desired outcome. Avoid corporate speak.

**RULE-008: Lead with Value**
Start with the reader's problem or desired outcome, not company history or vague statements.

**RULE-009: Make CTAs Obvious**
The call-to-action should be clear, early, and repeated. Don't bury it after too much buildup.

**RULE-010: Test and Iterate**
A/B test headlines, CTAs, value propositions, and page structure. Measure conversion impact.

## Quick Reference

### Quick-Pass Editing Checks

**Cut these words:**
- Just, really, very, actually, basically
- Utilize → use
- In order to → to
- At this point in time → now
- Nominalizations (verb → noun: "make a decision" → "decide")

**Sentence-Level:**
- One idea per sentence
- Active voice preferred
- No more than 25 words (usually)

**Paragraph-Level:**
- One topic per paragraph
- 3-5 sentences max
- White space is your friend

### Common Copy Problems & Fixes

| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Wall of Features | Add "which means..." after each feature |
| Corporate Speak | Ask "How would a human say this?" |
| Weak Opening | Lead with reader's problem or desired outcome |
| Buried CTA | Make CTA obvious, early, and repeated |
| No Proof | Add specific testimonials, numbers, case references |
| Generic Claims | Specify who, how, and by how much |
| Mixed Audiences | Pick one audience and write directly to them |
| Feature Overload | Focus on 3-5 key benefits that matter most |

### Page Structure Framework

**Homepage:**
1. Hero (value prop + CTA)
2. Social proof
3. How it works (3 steps)
4. Key benefits (3-5)
5. More social proof
6. FAQ
7. Final CTA

**Landing Page:**
1. Headline matching ad/source
2. Subheadline (expand on promise)
3. Hero image/video
4. Benefits (not features)
5. Social proof
6. How it works
7. Objection handling
8. Strong CTA

**Feature Page:**
1. Feature name + one-line benefit
2. Visual demonstration
3. Use cases
4. Comparison to alternatives
5. Customer stories
6. CTA to try
