## Overview

---

## Rules

**RULE-001: Email Sequence Strategy**
Design sequences with clear goals: onboarding, nurture, conversion, retention, or re-engagement. Each email should have one primary purpose.

**RULE-002: Subject Line Best Practices**
- Keep under 50 characters (mobile preview)
- Create curiosity without clickbait
- Personalize when possible
- A/B test consistently
- Avoid spam triggers (FREE, !!!, ALL CAPS)

**RULE-003: Email Structure**
Follow proven structure:
1. Preheader (extend subject line)
2. Personal greeting
3. Hook (why they should read)
4. Value/content
5. Clear CTA (one primary action)
6. P.S. (reinforce or add urgency)

**RULE-004: Personalization Hierarchy**
- Level 1: Name, company
- Level 2: Behavior-based (clicked, opened, purchased)
- Level 3: Segment-specific content
- Level 4: Dynamic content blocks
- Level 5: AI-generated personalization

**RULE-005: Segmentation Strategies**
Segment by:
- Engagement level (active, inactive, dormant)
- Customer lifecycle stage (trial, paid, churned)
- Behavior (feature usage, content interests)
- Demographics (company size, role, industry)
- Purchase history (product, value, frequency)

**RULE-006: Email Timing Optimization**
- Onboarding: Immediate, then 1 day, 3 days, 7 days, 14 days
- Nurture: Weekly or bi-weekly
- Promotional: Test send times (Tuesday-Thursday 10am-2pm often best)
- Re-engagement: 30, 60, 90 days of inactivity

**RULE-007: Mobile-First Design**
- 60%+ of emails opened on mobile
- Single column layout
- Large, tappable CTAs (44x44px minimum)
- Concise copy
- Images load fast or have alt text

**RULE-008: Testing Framework**
Test systematically:
- Subject lines (A/B test every send)
- Send time (test by segment)
- CTA copy and placement
- Email length (short vs. long)
- Personalization elements

**RULE-009: Deliverability Fundamentals**
- Authenticate (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
- Maintain list hygiene (remove bounces, inactive)
- Avoid spam triggers
- Monitor sender reputation
- Provide easy unsubscribe

**RULE-010: Metrics That Matter**
Track:
- Open rate (subject line effectiveness)
- Click-through rate (content relevance)
- Conversion rate (CTA effectiveness)
- Unsubscribe rate (content fit)
- Revenue per email (ROI)

## Quick Reference

### Email Sequence Templates

**Onboarding Sequence (SaaS):**
```
Email 1 (Immediate): Welcome + Quick start guide
Email 2 (Day 1): First win - achieve one outcome
Email 3 (Day 3): Feature spotlight - key capability
Email 4 (Day 7): Social proof - customer success story
Email 5 (Day 14): Upgrade prompt - premium features
Email 6 (Day 21): Check-in - how can we help?
```

**Nurture Sequence (B2B):**
```
Email 1: Educational content - solve a problem
Email 2: Case study - show results
Email 3: Product demo - show how it works
Email 4: Comparison - vs. alternatives
Email 5: Trial offer - remove risk
Email 6: Urgency - limited time/spots
```

**Re-engagement Sequence:**
```
Email 1 (30 days): "We miss you" - what changed?
Email 2 (45 days): Value reminder - what you're missing
Email 3 (60 days): Special offer - come back incentive
Email 4 (90 days): Final notice - stay or unsubscribe
```

### Subject Line Formulas

**Curiosity:**
- "The [X] mistake that's costing you [Y]"
- "Why [unexpected thing] is actually [surprising result]"
- "What [authority figure] gets wrong about [topic]"

**Benefit:**
- "How to [achieve outcome] in [timeframe]"
- "[Number] ways to [solve problem]"
- "Get [desired result] without [pain point]"

**Urgency:**
- "Last chance: [offer] ends [date]"
- "Only [X] spots left for [thing]"
- "[Time-sensitive opportunity]"

**Personal:**
- "[Name], quick question about [topic]"
- "I noticed you [action], here's [resource]"
- "Your [thing] is ready"

### Email Copy Guidelines

**Opening lines that work:**
- Ask a question
- Share a surprising stat
- Tell a quick story
- Acknowledge their situation
- Make a bold statement

**CTA best practices:**
- Use action verbs (Get, Start, Try, Download)
- Create urgency (Today, Now, Limited)
- Remove friction (Free, No credit card, 2-minute setup)
- Make it stand out (Button, color contrast)
- Repeat if email is long

**P.S. tactics:**
- Reinforce main CTA
- Add social proof
- Create urgency
- Offer bonus
- Ask question to drive reply

### Automation Triggers

**Behavior-based:**
- Signed up → Onboarding sequence
- Clicked link → Follow-up content
- Abandoned cart → Recovery sequence
- Trial ending → Upgrade prompt
- Inactive 30 days → Re-engagement

**Milestone-based:**
- First purchase → Thank you + next steps
- Anniversary → Celebration + offer
- Renewal date → Reminder + upgrade
- Usage milestone → Congratulations + share
